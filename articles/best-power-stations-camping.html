How to Choose a Power Station for Camping

Capacity (Wh) — How Much Power Do You Need?

The capacity is measured in watt-hours (Wh) and determines how long your power station will last. Here is a simple guide:

  • 300-500Wh: Weekend trips, phone and camera charging
  • 500-1000Wh: Extended trips, laptops, LED lights, small fans
  • 1000-2000Wh: Family camping, portable fridges, CPAP machines
  • 2000Wh+: Large groups, multiple high-power devices

Weight — Can You Carry It?

Power and portability are always a trade-off. A 2000Wh station gives you tons of power but weighs over 60 lbs. Think about how far you need to carry it from your car to your campsite. For car camping, weight matters less. For anything involving hiking, stay under 20 lbs.

Output (Watts) — What Can It Run?

Check the wattage of the devices you want to power. A 300W output can handle phones, cameras, and laptops. You need 1000W+ for things like electric kettles, blenders, or small heaters. Always choose a power station with at least 20% more output than your highest-wattage device.

Battery Type — LiFePO4 vs Li-ion

LiFePO4 batteries last 3000-4000+ charge cycles compared to 500-800 for standard lithium-ion. They are safer, more stable, and better for long-term investment. In 2025, we strongly recommend choosing a LiFePO4 model unless weight is your absolute top priority.

Solar Compatibility

If you plan to recharge via solar panels, pay attention to the maximum solar input wattage. Higher input means faster solar charging. A 200W solar input with a matching panel can typically charge a 1000Wh station in about 5-6 hours of good sunlight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a power station inside my tent?

Yes. Unlike gas generators, portable power stations produce zero emissions and are completely safe to use indoors or inside a tent. They are also much quieter.

How long will a power station run a portable fridge?

A typical 12V camping fridge draws about 40-50W. A 1000Wh power station can run it for approximately 17-20 hours. A 500Wh unit will give you about 8-10 hours.

Can I charge a power station with my car?

Yes, most power stations include a car charging cable that plugs into your 12V cigarette lighter socket. However, car charging is usually slower than wall charging. Charge times typically range from 5-12 hours depending on the model.

Are portable power stations worth the money?

If you camp regularly, absolutely. A good power station lasts 10+ years with LiFePO4 technology, provides clean silent power, and eliminates the need for noisy gas generators. The convenience and versatility easily justify the investment for frequent campers.

What size solar panel do I need?

Match the solar panel wattage to your power station solar input. For example, if your station accepts up to 200W solar input, get a 200W portable solar panel. For faster charging, you can connect multiple panels in series if your station supports higher input.
